Amanda Wright is a sports and entertainment industry professional with more than 10 years of experience in guest services, event management, and security operations for stadiums, arenas and performing arts facilities. She has worked in a variety of complex organizational settings including university, municipal government, private firm, and major league sports franchises. Amanda has been instructing for NCS4 for the last four years. During this time, she has been serving as a lead instructor in the NCS4 training program to provide training that enhances multi-agency collaboration when planning and preparing for large special events. Prior to instructing for NCS4, she worked for the Dallas Cowboys at AT&T Stadium in the Event Operations and Security department managing more than 1,000 guest services and security employees. Some of the events include the Inaugural College Football Playoff National Championship, the 50th Anniversary of the Academy of Country Music Awards, 2014 NCAA Men’s Final Four, and WrestleMania 32.

Amanda is a key member of the NCS4 training program and consistently evaluated as a top performer during her training deliveries.

  • Current Organization:

    Independent Consultant

  • What courses do you teach?

    AWR167 Sport Event Risk Management & MGT412 Sport Venue Evacuation and Protective Actions (and the 2 New Courses starting next year)

  • What motivated you to become and NCS4 Trainer?

    I love making the best better! Working for the Dallas Cowboys, that was a part of the culture I loved. I truly believe no matter how amazing you are, how great your system is, or how many times you have hosted that exact same event, you can ALWAYS do better! No single person knows everything... learning and improving is what pushes us forward and makes us stronger, more prepared, and more successful with our events. At the end of the day, I want to make sure the people that come to your event, no matter how big or small your attendance number is, every single one of those people need to make it back home safely from your event. I don’t want anyone to look back and say I wish I had done more…I’m here to help them do just a little bit more.

  • What has been the greatest professional benefit to instructing with NCS4?

    The relationships and knowledge I have personally gained are huge benefits. Not only do I enjoy teaching these courses, but I enjoy constantly learning from the students’ experiences that are discussed in every course – their tips, tricks, best practices, and even failures. I am becoming a better instructor and industry professional with every single class because of the people that attend the courses and share their experiences.

  • What is your most memorable location or experience from a delivery?

    There are two experiences that stand out because they are similar. We had the windows open in the classroom in Santa Rosa, California and while I was leading the class in a discussion suddenly, I was interrupted by a strange noise… Gobble-gobble!! There were 5 turkeys gobbling right outside those windows in the parking lot! Apparently, turkeys roaming around the city was normal to the locals but a unique experience for me! Similarly, I was instructing in Anchorage, Alaska and we took a break between modules; the class opened one of the outside venue doors and immediately yelled for me to come over. Soon as I did, they opened the door to show me a huge moose standing not 3 feet from the door!! For them, this was not unusual, for me… I was amazed!

  • What is one takeaway from any NCS4 course you wish you knew as a young professional?

    I wish I had known this training was available!! I had no idea there were so many courses that I could take for free that would have greatly helped me in my early years in this industry. When I first attended an NCS4 course I was impressed at all the relevant information I was receiving and was slightly upset no one had told me about these courses sooner!

  • Why do you feel these courses are important to the security industry and the nation?

    There’s a statement in one of the videos of these courses that says, “What if the worse thing happened in your venue today, what will you have wished you would have done? That’s what you must be doing now. Because how do you know today isn’t the worst day?” Since the first time I heard that phrase, it has stuck with me. It is exactly why everyone that puts on any type or size of event needs to take these courses. There is no 100% safe venue; the threat is real and present anywhere, anytime. Life safety must always be the number one priority and these courses help to prepare and prevent those incidents.


"Tangible takeaways are always my goal with a class; I want every class member to leave the course with something they can confidently take back to their organization and start implementing. These courses give them the opportunity to learn new ideas, look at situations differently, and communicate more effectively with their event partners. Participating in these courses is an invaluable part of making an organization’s events better and safer." - Amanda Wright
